Brief

Redesign the UI of an already existing software based on user research and make the changes as per the user needs.

Problem Statement

To solve the over complex UI of steam.

Pain Points:

  • One of the key takeaways was the importance of feedback and clear communication within the interface. By ensuring that users are consistently informed about the system's status and providing clear cues for navigation and interaction, we can enhance usability and reduce cognitive load.
  • Moreover, heuristic evaluation highlighted the value of simplicity and consistency in design. By streamlining interfaces and maintaining consistency in layout, terminology, and visual elements, we can minimize user confusion and enhance learnability.
  • Additionally, understanding the significance of user control and freedom underscored the importance of empowering users to navigate the interface at their own pace, without feeling constrained or restricted.
  • Overcomplicated UI which was developed over time for more seasoned users. Not being considerate for the newer users.

Solutions:

  • After narrowing down our target audience we started our user testing. We all took turns taking down the metrics of every user, we had a different spokesperson, timekeeper & metrics collectors.
  • After our testing, we found out that even the best of the best software's and apps also have flaws though these companies have done in-depth research.
  • We figured that these apps are usually developed for specific demographics.
  • This helped strengthen our final report and our modified Hi-Fi screens.
  • Since we were doing the user test on "Steam" and most of the users had a bit or good knowledge of the UI, they almost all found it very simple to use.
